Interventions

Major interventions.

My key areas: My work so far predominantly focused on torture, forced eviction, caste violence, reprisal against human rights defenders, etc. My mode of interventions include rehabilitative and psychosocial support to victims, prosecution of state agents and violators of human rights through legal interventions. My scope of engagement begins with grassroots to engaging with the NHRC & UN bodies.

Forced eviction in Mullai Nagar, Madurai, India

Mullai Nagar residents begin indefinite protest opposing eviction They say moving to a far away place will be a big impediment in earning their livelihoods; they also have to pay ₹1.5 lakh each for the new houses promised by the govt.

Forced eviction in Manjolai – Intervention before the Supreme Court of India

'Manjolai Samathuvapuram' creation sought for rehabilitation of workers They have agreed for rehabilitation measures but want a better alternative and sought a compensation of ₹25 lakh for relocation, says advocate

Custodial death of Gokul Shree (Juvenile) – legal intervention and advocacy

MADURAI: People's Watch, a human rights NGO, asked the Tamil Nadu government to consider the case of GokulSri, a juvenile who died at an observation home, as a wake-up call and set up a special committee to inspect all the observation homes across the state on Wednesday.

Whom did I work for ?

Victims of Torture

Most of our victims of torture and reprisal belong to vulnerable groups and economically deprived sections. They live in the margins of the society belonging to the Dalit and tribal communities, including the nomadic tribes criminalized under the Criminal Tribes Act, 1870. They often go unrecognized with lacking state will with limited avenues for redressal and reparation.
